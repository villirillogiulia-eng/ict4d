Project Description

Strengthening community-level disaster preparedness in The Gambia's North Bank Region through the NBR Resilience Sentinel.

Our Vision

The NBR Resilience Sentinel was born from a simple but urgent realization: national-level climate data is often too broad to be useful for local action. While national reports might predict a drought for the entire country, our field teams need to know exactly which villages in the North Bank Region will be hardest hit and which health facilities are most vulnerable.

Our vision is to transform CLI’s field operations into a data-empowered "Mission Control" where local expertise is amplified by high-resolution geospatial intelligence.

Methodology

Our approach is built on three main pillars of analysis:

  • Infrastructure Vulnerability Mapping: We use OpenStreetMap data to map every health facility in the NBR and calculate its specific risk profile based on proximity to historical flood zones.
  • Historical Hazard Analysis: By processing decades of data from the Global Flood Archive, we identify recurring hazard patterns that simple forecasts often miss.
  • Future Climate Projections: We integrate 2026-2030 and 2050 climate models to ensure that today's preparedness plans remain effective decades into the future.

The AI Difference

The Sentinel utilizes AI-driven automated monitoring to scan satellite feeds and climate data at a scale that would be impossible for manual analysis. This allows CLI to shift from reactive disaster response to proactive resilience building.

Strategic Impact

The North Bank Region is our proving ground. By successfully bridging the granularity gap here, we are creating a blueprint for climate-resilient development that can be scaled across the entire Gambia and the broader West African region.

This project is not just about building a dashboard; it’s about creating a permanent asset for CLI. Through our Train-the-Trainer (ToT) program, we ensure that the technical capacity to maintain and evolve this system remains within the community it serves.

Implementation Roadmap

  • Phase 1: Data Ingestion & Risk Scoring (Completed)
  • Phase 2: Deployment of the Resilience Sentinel Dashboard (Live)
  • Phase 3: Field Team Training & Capacity Transfer (Starting Q3 2026)
  • Phase 4: Expansion to Central River & Upper River Regions (Planned 2027)
